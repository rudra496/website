This is how we built a high-performance bionic arm for $500.

1. Dual-Sensor Fusion

EMG + SMG = Unmatched Control

Traditional prosthetics often rely only on Electromyography (EMG), which detects electrical signals from muscles. This is effective, but can be disrupted by sweat, skin impedance, or electrode displacement.

Our innovation is fusing EMG with **Sonomyography (SMG)**, an ultrasound technology that measures the *mechanical deformation* (shape, thickness) of the muscle as it contracts. By combining the rapid *temporal* data of EMG with the reliable *spatial* data of SMG, we achieve superior pattern recognition and filter out false activations.

2. The 89.7% Power-Save

The Revolutionary Spring-Assisted Grip

A major user complaint about existing prosthetics is battery life. Traditional designs consume power *continuously* to hold a grip.

Our novel mechanism provides gripping force through a **spring**. The motor is only activated for a few seconds to *open* the hand against the spring. This simple change reduces total energy consumption by approximately **89.7%** during common tasks, allowing for multi-day battery life. 

E_traditional = (P_max * 2s) + (0.3 * P_max * 58s) = 19.4 P_max
E_proposed    = (P_max * 2s)                        =  2.0 P_max

Reduction = (17.4 / 19.4) * 100 = 89.7%

3. Feel & Affordability

Haptic Feedback and Smart Sensor Choice

To make the arm feel like a natural extension, we integrated small **vibration motors**. As the user tightens their grip, the motors vibrate, simulating the sensation of pressure and touch. Patients in our trials reported this was a key feature for improving control and convenience.

To meet our $500 price point, we analyzed 353 user reviews for 9 different sensors. We selected the **MyoWare Muscle Sensor V3** for its perfect balance of cost-effectiveness ($25), technical efficiency, and user-friendly built-in electrodes.
